## THE PROFITABILITY FILTER (all 3 legs required)
Profitable = HIGH Pinterest demand  ×  CONTENT GAP (weak supply)  ×  MONETIZABLE
Miss any leg = dead. Most niches fail on GAP (saturated) or MONETIZE (no real
program / buyer too cheap).

## THE #1 LESSON — OPERATOR vs BROWSER TEST (run FIRST, it's the fastest kill)
Pinterest reliably delivers ONE audience: young (≈85% 18-34), ≈87% female,
ASPIRING creators who buy CHEAP digital/physical products. The high-value money
(operators, pros, B2B) is NOT here. So before anything: does this term's audience
= paying operators or aspiring browsers? If browsers, only cheap-product + ad
models work — never high-ticket affiliate / SaaS.

## METHOD A — LIVE PINTEREST PROBE (fast, free, do this first)
1. AUTOCOMPLETE: pinterest.com search, type "seed " (+space) → harvest
   suggestions = real long-tail demand + the buyer's exact words. Untapped long-
   tail hides here.
2. GUIDED BUBBLES: the refinement chips on a results page appear only on HIGH-
   volume terms and reveal the demand sub-segments (audience/use-case cuts).
3. TRENDS KEYWORD TOOL (trends.pinterest.com → Search trends, region=US):
   - Is the term even TRACKED? (no autocomplete / "unavailable for region" = low
     volume = weak.)
   - Interest-over-time index 0-100 + seasonality + "Predict the future".
   - DEMOGRAPHICS (age + gender) = the operator-vs-browser verdict + buyer profile.
4. SERP / SUPPLY read: search the term — are top pins real teaching blogs, cheap
   product pins, or junk? Fresh or dated? Sponsored pins present (= commercial
   value)? Big established accounts (hard) vs small/new (winnable)?
   - Pattern seen: "[X] for [specific person/use-case]" long-tail = the gap;
     head terms = saturated.

## METHOD B — BUYER-PSYCHOLOGY DEEP DIVE (agent)
Who buys (segment ladder — find the slice with money+pain+intent), pain RANKED by
intensity (Reddit/forums/communities, not marketing), what they ALREADY pay for
(price anchors + WTP ceiling), purchase TRIGGERS + OBJECTIONS, exact LANGUAGE,
product-fit verdict. Flag if the real buyer is a thin/locked-in slice.

## METHOD C — SATURATION KILL-TEST (adversarial agent — brief it to KILL the idea)
Who owns the space (incumbents + SaaS-funded blogs that are ALSO your SEO rivals
+ marketplace sellers). Channel fortification. Honest "too late" case. Concede
white space only if it genuinely survives. (Short-form video = best newcomer
break-in 2026; Google SEO collapsing under AI Overviews; Pinterest declining.)

## METHOD D — MONETIZATION REALITY CHECK (agent — verify, don't trust blogs)
For each program: REAL cash affiliate or just account credit? recurring vs ONE-
TIME? payout GATE / approval bar / cookie? Most "affiliate programs" = referral
credit or one-time gated bounties. Digital-product economics: price, demand-vs-
competition by product TYPE. Verdict: is money in affiliate, own-product, or
neither?

## METHOD E — ADVERSARIAL STRESS TEST (before committing)
Spawn agents told to DESTROY the thesis; attack the load-bearing assumptions
(is demand real? is the behavior real? does the funnel work? does the money
exist?). Survives a real attack → build. Cracks → pivot. No sunk-cost loyalty.

## HARD-WON RULES
- Validate the AUDIENCE-CHANNEL fit BEFORE building. (Cost us the Etsy detour.)
- Sell the OUTCOME, never "a template." Match the format the buyer already uses
  (Sheets/Canva > Notion for non-technical creatives).
- Affiliate is GARNISH on a $0 Pinterest play, not a pillar (one-time + gated).
- The constraint is usually the VEHICLE (channel+monetization), not the niche.
