# FLOW STATE — ONLINE MASTER PLAN
_The complete plan: thesis → affiliate foundation → content → keywords → operations → launch._
_Status: working draft (v1, 2026-07-02). Nothing here is sacred — follow the evidence, pivot freely._

---

## 1. THESIS & POSITIONING

**Flow State, scaled national online** — the honest, conscious, science-grounded rave authority the
scene doesn't have. A Boiler Room structure: the **SLC events stay the soul + proof you actually throw
them**; the **online brand becomes the national community, the reach, and the income.**

The gap it fills: rave media splits into two conflicted camps — **retailer blogs** that only exist to
sell their own apparel, and **music-news sites** that ignore the actual experience. Nobody credible owns
the trusted, independent, values-first lane. Flow State already _is_ that voice. The one small independent
using Pinterest as a blog engine (Rave Hackers, ~28k) is under-resourced and unchallenged. "That seat is empty."

**Positioning line:** the "Resident Advisor / Wirecutter of the rave scene" — powered by real events.

## 2. ARCHITECTURE — one brand, two arms

| | **Flow State — Events** (the soul) | **Flow State — Online** (the reach) |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | SLC underground, local, physical | National, digital, community + media |
| Role | Real rooms → credibility; proprietary content engine | Owned audience the events tap; the income |
| Money | Tickets, local | Affiliate + ads → brand deals |

**The flywheel:** Content (Pinterest/TikTok/IG) → Owned community (email/Discord) → Events + Brand deals →
Proprietary content (BTS, aftermovies, access generic bloggers can't make) → back to content.
Merch + affiliate + ads run across every stage.

**Cercle's lesson (2026):** a huge free audience did NOT save them — keep a hard revenue layer
(merch, deals, ticket margin) under the free content.

**The build direction (bottom-up):** start at **affiliate + blogging** (the $0 entry, near-term money),
build UP toward brand deals (the destination + the real income-replacement lever). Affiliate/ads are the
FLOOR that funds & proves the build; brand deals + community + events replace the income.

- **Target sub-tribe: EDM / MAINSTAGE RAVER** (maximalist — neon, kandi, LED). Biggest scene (EDC Vegas
  525k+), female outfit-buying core, $50-150/outfit, mature affiliate ecosystem. + house, + trance/Burning
  Man as high-AOV seasonal satellites. **SKIP techno** (anti-photo, anti-costume) except as culture content.

## 4. THE ALIGNMENT FILTER (the law that governs everything)

> **"Is this something Flow State would do itself?"** Every partner, product, and affiliate passes this
> test or it's out — no matter what it pays. This is what lets the brand run affiliate/sponsorships without
> going salesy: the community can _feel_ every rec is real. The filter isn't a limit on the business — it IS the moat.

**Would:** Loop/Eargasm earplugs (protect the crowd), hydration + durable/reusable gear, test kits/
harm-reduction, aligned conscious rave-wear, quality re-wearable pieces.
**Wouldn't:** SHEIN/fast-fashion wear-once fits, generic mainstream shilling, cash-grab brands, woo/wellness
framing, VIP-bottle-service energy, anything you wouldn't book or wear yourself.

## 8. PINTEREST OPERATING MANUAL (the essentials)

- **Fresh pins = the #1 lever.** A NEW IMAGE on the same URL = ~64-77% of a brand-new pin's reach. One post
  → 5-15 fresh pins over weeks, spaced 3-7 days, on the most-relevant board. You don't need endless posts.
- **Two metrics:** SAVES (top ranking signal — design to be saved) + OUTBOUND CLICKS (the only money metric).
  Ignore impressions/monthly-views.
- **Pins:** static 2:3 (1000×1500) linked images = the click engine; video = reach layer. Alt text on every
  pin. HIGH-CONTRAST / light-background pins win the data (⚠️ tension with the dark brand — give PINS a
  brighter treatment, keep the blog core dark, test it).
- **SEO:** keywords in pin title (front-load) + description + board name (exact-keyword, not cute). Research
  via Pinterest autocomplete + guided tiles + Trends. Hashtags dead; first-comment stuffing = myth.
- **Affiliate mechanics:** direct links allowed "in moderation" + disclosed + no cloaking; Amazon needs your
  Pinterest registered + no masked links. PREFER the bridge (pin → full blog POST → affiliate link) — but it
  must be a REAL value-added post, not a thin doorway page (Google penalizes those).
- **FTC:** disclosure must be unavoidable, before-the-click, plain language ("#ad" or a sentence) — in the
  pin description AND at the top of the post, near the link. Not footer/bio/hashtag-clump.
- **Account safety:** ONE clean domain, native links, no cloaking, no bulk-identical pins. Domain blocks are
  the catastrophic risk (appeals unreliable) — protect it. Tailwind is an approved partner; bots get banned.
- **New-account ramp:** near-zero 4 weeks, pins index 45-60 days, traction 3-6 months, REAL money year 2.
  Safe cadence: 3-5 fresh pins/day.
- **Myths to ignore:** "16,000 signals / algorithm override system," "Idea pins can't link" (obsolete 2023),
  "rich pins boost ranking," exact daily pin caps. All guru/course-funnel junk.

## 9. STACK & MONETIZATION EXECUTION

- **Blog:** self-hosted WordPress.org + managed host (only thing that runs real ad networks).
- **Affiliate links:** Pretty Links (cheap start) → Lasso once revenue justifies.
- **Email:** MailerLite → Kit. **Scheduling:** Tailwind (~$18/mo) or native. **Analytics:** GA4 + Search Console.
- **Ads ladder:** AdSense → Journey by Mediavine (1,000 sessions = first real money) → Raptive (25k pageviews)
  → Mediavine. RPM $20-40 at premium tiers; model conservatively.
- **Content that converts:** "best X" roundups, "X vs Y," reviews, packing lists — written for Google
  buyer-intent first, pinned second. **AI for scaffolding only, never the ranking words** (HCU hit 71% of
  affiliate sites — you write the experience/opinion).
- **The revenue order:** brand deals → community/products → affiliate → ads (floor). Ads are the base load
  once traffic scales; affiliate is the swing; brand deals + events are the income-replacement engine.

**The winner-vs-loser line:** run three channels — Pinterest (ignition, months 1-12) + Google SEO
(compounding, year 2+) + EMAIL (owned insurance, from day one). Single-channel dependence kills the 95%.

## 11. GUARDRAILS & HONEST EXPECTATIONS

- **Seasonal** (Feb-Oct traffic; publish evergreen + community to smooth winter).

  company de-risks this — the content doesn't have to carry the business.
- **Diversify** — Pinterest is deliberately keeping users on-platform; lean on email + Google + TikTok/IG too.
- **Solo for now** — build lean, systematize with AI, revisit help once the flywheel turns.
